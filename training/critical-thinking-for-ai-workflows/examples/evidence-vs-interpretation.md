# Example: Evidence vs Interpretation

This example uses fictional source notes to practice separating source-backed facts, interpretations, assumptions, open questions, risks, decisions, and follow-up actions.

## Synthetic Source Notes

The review group discussed whether to move a draft intake process into a small pilot. The notes say the group "agreed to try the pilot with one sample team next month if the owner confirms capacity." A participant said the current intake form is "probably too long" and may discourage completion. Another participant suggested that a short checklist could reduce confusion, but no one confirmed who would maintain it. The group noted that reporting expectations are unclear. A follow-up was proposed to ask the process owner whether the pilot scope is acceptable.

These notes are fictional and do not describe a real organization, person, project, or meeting.

## Classification Table

| Statement | Category | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The group discussed moving a draft intake process into a small pilot. | Source-backed fact | The source notes directly state this discussion happened. |
| The group approved the pilot. | Interpretation requiring review | The notes say the group agreed to try the pilot only if capacity is confirmed. Approval is conditional and not fully established. |
| The current intake form may discourage completion. | Risk | The source includes a concern that the form is probably too long and may reduce completion. |
| The intake form is definitely too long. | Unsupported overclaim | The source says "probably too long," not a confirmed measurement. |
| A short checklist could reduce confusion. | Interpretation | This is a proposed improvement, not a proven result. |
| Someone must maintain the checklist. | Assumption | Maintenance is implied by the proposed checklist, but ownership is not confirmed. |
| Who owns checklist maintenance? | Open question | The notes explicitly say no one confirmed who would maintain it. |
| Ask the process owner whether pilot scope is acceptable. | Follow-up action candidate | The source says this was proposed, but it still requires acceptance and ownership before becoming an approved action. |
| Reporting expectations are unclear. | Source-backed fact | The notes directly state this gap. |

## Review Questions

- Which statements are directly supported by the notes?
- Which statements are interpretations that need labeling?
- Which assumptions would need confirmation?
- Which open questions should block approval?
- Which items are risks rather than recommendations?
- Which follow-up actions need an owner before they become accepted?

## Governance Lesson

The same source notes can produce useful reviewable artifacts, but those artifacts are not automatically true, complete, or approved. Human review is required to decide what becomes an approved record or action.
