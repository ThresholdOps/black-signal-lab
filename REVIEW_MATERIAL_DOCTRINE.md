# Black Signal Lab Review Material Doctrine

## Purpose

This document defines the doctrine that AI-assisted output is review material, not final truth.

It explains the status of AI output, the responsibility of reviewers, human decision ownership, and what must not be automated.

The doctrine is practical. It is meant to guide how artifacts are reviewed before they influence records, decisions, handoffs, publication, escalation, or action.

## Doctrine Anchor

AI output is not truth.

AI output is not a decision.

AI output is review material.

This is the review boundary.

No generated output becomes reliable, approved, actionable, or authoritative only because it is fluent, structured, confident, or produced by a model.

## Status of AI Output

AI-assisted output starts as provisional material.

It may be useful. It may be well structured. It may be directionally correct.

It is still not approved.

Default status:

```text
AI output = review material
```

It may become something else only after review:

```text
review material -> corrected artifact -> accepted artifact -> approved handoff or decision support
```

The transition requires human review and explicit status change.

## What Review Material Means

Review material is output prepared for inspection.

It is not useless. It is not automatically wrong. It is simply not final.

Review material may include:

- summaries,
- classifications,
- extracted decisions,
- evidence maps,
- risk notes,
- interpretation reports,
- task contracts,
- handoff notes,
- validation results,
- public-safe examples,
- candidate recommendations.

Review material must remain labeled, bounded, and inspectable until a human reviewer accepts, corrects, rejects, escalates, or defers it.

## Reviewer Responsibility

A reviewer is responsible for deciding what the artifact is allowed to mean.

A reviewer should check:

- whether the input boundary is clear,
- whether the output matches its purpose,
- whether claims are supported,
- whether assumptions are visible,
- whether gaps are visible,
- whether sensitive or private material is excluded,
- whether the artifact overclaims its status,
- whether the next use is allowed,
- whether a decision owner is explicit.

Review is not passive reading.

Review is an accountability action.

## Human Decision Ownership

AI can prepare decision material.

AI does not own decisions.

A decision requires a human or authorized body that can accept responsibility for consequence.

The method separates:

- model output,
- reviewable artifact,
- validation result,
- reviewer judgment,
- decision ownership,
- approved action,
- stored record.

No model output should be treated as an approved decision unless a human decision point is explicit and recorded.

## What Must Not Be Automated

The following should not be fully automated in Black Signal Lab public methodology:

- final approval of decisions,
- acceptance of business or operational consequence,
- publication of sensitive or boundary-risk material,
- escalation of high-impact issues without human review,
- assignment of real-world ownership without approval,
- conversion of draft output into approved record,
- replacement of source-of-truth records,
- determination that private material is safe to publish,
- removal of human accountability from a workflow,
- claims of production readiness,
- claims that validation proves truth.

Automation may support preparation, structure, checking, comparison, and routing.

Automation must not replace accountable judgment.

## Review Outcomes

A review should end with an explicit outcome.

Common outcomes:

```text
accept
correct
reject
defer
escalate
store as draft
store as approved artifact
approve for handoff
```

Each outcome should preserve status.

A corrected artifact is not the same as an approved artifact.

A validation pass is not the same as acceptance.

A stored draft is not the same as an approved record.

## Escalation Rules

Escalation is required when review material contains or implies:

- high-impact decisions,
- legal, compliance, or policy exposure,
- private or sensitive material,
- uncertain source boundaries,
- unsupported but consequential claims,
- potential reputational harm,
- operational changes,
- public publication risk,
- unclear ownership,
- conflict between artifact and source of truth.

Escalation does not mean the artifact is wrong.

It means the reviewer should not decide alone.

## Public-Safe Wording

Public artifacts should use status language that prevents overclaiming.

Prefer:

- review material,
- draft,
- candidate interpretation,
- source-backed claim,
- assumption,
- open question,
- validation result,
- human-reviewed artifact,
- approved handoff,
- deferred item.

Avoid wording that implies unsupported authority:

- final truth,
- verified by AI,
- automatically approved,
- fully validated,
- production-ready,
- autonomous decision,
- confirmed root cause without review,
- official record without approval.

Public wording should make the boundary visible.

## Relationship to Method Principles

This doctrine supports the six method principles:

- Artifact Principle: output must become inspectable work before it can be useful.
- Review Principle: output remains provisional until reviewed.
- Evidence Principle: claims should remain connected to support.
- Human Decision Principle: consequence remains human-owned.
- Boundary Principle: input and handoff limits must be explicit.
- Anti-Overclaim Principle: artifact status must not be inflated.

The doctrine is the hard edge of those principles.

## Relationship to Artifact Lifecycle

In the artifact lifecycle, this doctrine mainly governs:

- validation gate,
- human review,
- decision gate,
- storage / handoff,
- improvement loop.

The lifecycle describes movement.

The doctrine defines what cannot be skipped.

## Minimal Review Checklist

Before using an AI-assisted artifact, ask:

- Is this still review material?
- Has a human reviewed it?
- Are the source boundaries clear?
- Are claims connected to evidence?
- Are assumptions visible?
- Are gaps visible?
- Does the artifact overclaim?
- Is the decision owner explicit?
- Is the next use allowed?
- Does anything require escalation?

If these questions cannot be answered, the artifact is not ready for trusted use.

## Doctrine Summary

AI can help produce structure.

AI can help surface patterns.

AI can help prepare artifacts.

AI can help check for gaps.

AI can help make review faster.

AI does not create truth by sounding confident.

AI does not accept responsibility.

AI does not replace the human decision point.

The work becomes useful when it becomes reviewable.

The work becomes accountable when a human accepts the consequence.
